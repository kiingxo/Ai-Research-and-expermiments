Overfitting happens when a machine learning model learns the training data too well — including the noise, outliers, or random fluctuations — instead of learning the underlying patterns that generalize to new, unseen data.

⸻

Example:

Imagine you’re training an AI to recognize cats in pictures.
	•	✅ If your model learns general patterns of cats (e.g., whiskers, fur, ears), it will perform well on new cat pictures it hasn’t seen.
	•	❌ But if it memorizes specific training images (e.g., background, exact lighting, cat’s collar), it might fail to recognize different cat pictures later.

This is overfitting: doing great on training data but poorly on new data.
